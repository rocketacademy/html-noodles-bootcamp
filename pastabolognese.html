Pasta Bolognese

Preparation time: 40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1 medium carrot, finely diced
  • 2 ounces pancetta, thickly sliced, finely diced
  • 1/2 pound ground beef
  • 3/4 cup dry white wine
  • 1 (28-ounce) can peeled Italian tomatoes, seeded and finely chopped, juices reserved
  • 1 cup chicken stock or canned low-sodium broth
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 2 pounds penne rigate
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of the olive oil in a large, heavy saucepan until shimmering. Add the onion, carrot, celery, and pancetta and cook over moderate heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the remaining 3 tablespoons of olive oil to the saucepan and heat until just shimmering. Add the beef, veal, and pork and cook over moderately high heat until just barely pink, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the tomatoes and their juices, the chicken stock, thyme, and bay leaf. Season with a generous pinch of salt and pepper and bring to a boil over high heat.
  5. Cover partially and cook over moderately low heat for 1 hour. Discard the bay leaf. Stir in the heavy cream and cook the sauce just until heated through.
  6. Serve the pasta in deep bowls and pass the Parmesan at the table.
