Char Kway Teow recipe

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces (225g) fresh rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large prawns (shrimp), peeled and deveined
  • 2 Chinese sausages (Lap Cheong), sliced
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon dark soy sauce (optional, for color)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • Chopped chives or green onions for garnish (optional)
  • Chili paste or sambal for extra heat (optional)
  • Lime wedges for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Soak the fresh rice noodles in warm water to separate them. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a wok or large skillet, heat the cooking oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Add the prawns and Chinese sausages to the wok. Stir-fry until the prawns turn pink and are almost cooked through.
  4. Push the prawns and sausages to one side of the wok, and pour the beaten eggs into the other side. Scramble the eggs until they are partially set.
  5. Combine the eggs with the prawns and sausages. Add the drained rice noodles and bean sprouts to the wok. Toss everything together.
  6. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce (if using), sugar, and any optional chili paste or sambal for extra heat. Pour this sauce over the noodles and stir-fry for a few more minutes until everything is well coated and heated through.
  7. Serve the Char Kway Teow hot, garnished with chopped chives or green onions, and lime wedges on the side if desired. Enjoy!
