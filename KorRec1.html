JAPCHAE: KOREAN STIR-FRIED GLASS NOODLES

Preparation time:

2 hours

INGREDIENTS

  • Sweet potato noodles
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Pepper
  • spinach
  • Shiitake mushrooms
  • Beef/Pork
  • Soy sauce
  • Gralic
  • Sugar
  • Sweet rice Wine
  • Oils
  • Sesame seeds

METHOD

  1. Soak the noodles in hot water for 15 minutes, then drain and set aside. Pre-soaking will soften the noodles and make them easier to cook.
  2. Slice beef (or pork) into thin match sticks and season with soy sauce, sugar, and rice wine; set aside.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine all the japchae sauce ingredients, and set aside.
  4. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet or wok over medium high heat. Add onion, carrot, pepper, and a pinch of salt; stir-fry until soft.
  5. Add the sliced shiitake mushrooms and another pinch of salt. Continue to stir-fry until they become soft. Stir-fry the spinach until it wilts, and add it at the end.
  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and transfer the vegetables to a large plate to cool.
  7. Reheat the pan over high heat with 1 tablespoon of oil, then add the beef (or pork) and stir-fry until fully cooked. You will see the juice coming out from the meat.
  8. Transfer the meat to the large plate with the reserved vegetables, reserving the juice in the pan.
  9. Drain the noodles from soaking and add them to the pan with the meat juices.
  10. Pour the japchae sauce over the noodles and toss to combine.
  11. Allow the noodles to cook over medium heat until they become soft and absorb most of the liquid from the sauce.
  12. Reduce the heat to low. Add the vegetables and meat back to the pan over the noodles.
  13. Add sesame seeds and toss all together to incorporate.
  14. Drizzle more sesame oil if you wish. Taste and season more according to your taste.
