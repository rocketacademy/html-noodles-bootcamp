Total time to prepare: 40 mins

    Ingredients

  • 300g Plain flour
  • 150g Caster sugar
  • 2Â½ tsp Baking powder
  • 250ml Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 2 Eggs large
  • 200g Butter melted

    Instuctions

  1. Play "Single Ladies" on repeat
  2. Put the butter into a saucepan and melt it over a low heat, remove from the heat and set aside
  3. Turn on your waffles maker so it can heat up, I put mine on the max heat setting, but all waffle makers are different
  4. In a large mixing bowl, add the plain flour, caster sugar and baking powder. Stir them together until well combined
  5. In another bowl, or a jug, add the milk, eggs and vanilla extract. Gently whisk them together until combined
  6. Pour the milk mixture into the flour mixture, whisking as you pour, and keep whisking until the batter is smooth
  7. Then add the melted butter bit by bit, whisking between each addition, until the batter is smooth and shiny
  8. I prefer to transfer the batter into a jug so I can easily pour it into my waffle maker, but you can also use a spoon to spoon it into the waffle maker as well
  9. Pour some of the batter into your waffle maker, you want the little squares to be covered but still just about visible through the batter. If you're not sure how much to, it's better to add too little and then increase the amount as you go. If the batter does spill out the sides don't worry, just wipe it away
  10. Gently close the lid of the waffle maker, do not push it down. The waffles will take around 4-5 minutes, but you can check on them from 3 minutes
  11. Once the waffle is cooked to your liking, carefully remove it from the waffle maker, I use silicone tongs to do this, and place on a cooling rack
  12. Repeat until all of the batter is used up
  13. The waffles will seem soft when you first remove them from the waffle maker, but they will crisp up quickly as they start to cool
  14. Serve immediately, or allow to cool and store in the fridge or freeze them to enjoy another time
