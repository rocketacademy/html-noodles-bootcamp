Kung Pao Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 3-4 dried red chili peppers
  • 1 tablespoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 3 scallions, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar

Preparation time:

20 minutes

Cooking time:

15 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the chicken, cornstarch, and 1 tablespoon of soy sauce. Set aside.
  2. In a wok or large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over high heat. Add the peanuts and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes, until golden brown. Remove from the wok and set aside.
  3. In the same wok, add the dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry for 10-20 seconds, until fragrant.
  4. Add the garlic, ginger, and scallions to the wok and stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  5. Add the chicken to the wok and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, until cooked through.
  6. Add the red and green bell peppers to the wok and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes.
  7. In a small bowl, whisk together the rice vinegar, soy sauce, and sugar. Pour the mixture over the chicken and vegetables and stir-fry for another minute.
  8. Add the roasted peanuts to the wok and stir-fry for another minute.
  9. Serve hot with rice.
